Setting up a Memory Cafè

Listen to this page

Setting up a Memory Café is a fairly simple process, but you will need a dedicated group of people who are committed to getting the Memory Café up and running. Once the Memory Café is established, it will almost run itself.

The first step is to arrange a meeting between key people in your community. You may include:

  • A café owner
  • Shopping centre management
  • A local government representative
  • A person living with dementia
  • A carer
  • And other interested community members

In the meeting, talk about how the Memory Café will look, feel and operate.

Your next step is to identify a suitable café. You may already have a café owner on board, or have an idea of a café you would like to approach.

Before deciding on a café, it’s a good idea to consider our dementia enabling environment guidelines, available to download from our website.

The guidelines cover things like parking, wheelchair access, noise levels, space and location.

One important outcome from the meeting should be the formation of a working group, which is just a group of people who will meet on a regular basis to help get the Memory Café up and running.

What is a Dementia Friendly Community?

A dementia friendly community can be defined as a place where people living with dementia are supported to live a high quality of life with meaning, purpose and value.

The community consultations undertaken as a part of the initial phase of the Dementia Friendly Communities Project in 2015, outlined the need for:

  • Reduction of stigma
  • Increased knowledge of dementia in primary health services
  • Built environments that incorporate dementia design
  • The awareness of increased needs of people living with dementia in businesses and services
  • Increased support and services for families
  • Increased availability of person-centered services
  • Improved access to social clubs and activities for people living with dementia.

Memory Cafés were established as a suggested method of addressing some of these issues, and to provide peer to peer support.

We encourage you to contact Alzheimer’s WA to assist you in establishing a Memory Café in your area and can provide relevant training to Local Governments, café owners and their staff, shopping centres and other interested organisations.

Process to set-up a Memory Café

  1. Initial meeting facilitated between relevant bodies (i.e. café, shopping centre, Local Governments, people living with dementia and wider community members) and Alzheimer’s WA

    Discussion to define:

    • What a Memory Café is - an informal and social meeting place for like-minded individuals going through a similar experience

    • The initial parameters – how the Memory Café will look, feel and operate

    • Development of a working group from the above people.

  2. Roles and responsibilities

    • A representative (i.e. Shire, Alzheimer’s WA or community) will try and be in attendance at each planning meeting/café event

    • Promotion is the responsibility of everyone involved

    • If any members or attendees require information about dementia, they will be referred in the first instance to Alzheimer’s WA

    • Resources about dementia will be made available by Alzheimer’s WA. Memory Café Guidelines

    • Other resources, events or activities that may be of interest can be provided by attendees and partners.

  3. Potential café identified
    Alzheimer’s WA and relevant community members confirm suitability using environment guidelines in this booklet

  4. Meeting held with the preferred/identified café management to confirm involvement in joining the Memory Café group.

  5. With guidance from Alzheimer’s WA, a day and frequency of the Memory Café will be established.

  6. Training staff - provided by Alzheimer’s WA (fees apply).
    Why?

    • ensuring a safe and inclusive environment for those that attend to provide a more meaningful experience

    • equipping staff with basic dementia knowledge and principles

  7. Define suitable and inclusive position within café

  8. Marketing and promotion developed

    • Invitational flyer and online content creation

    • Organise launch event


    Alzheimer’s WA will list the Memory Café on our website, promote the launch on social media and member communications.

    Alzheimer’s WA can provide advice and recommendations to ensure dementia friendly enabling environments.

    Alzheimer’s WA can also provide advice on inviting media to the Memory Café as required.

    Once approved by all involved, material to be promoted through all networks. Any use of the Alzheimer’s WA logo requires formal approval.

  9. Launch / Opening
  10. Please contact Alzheimer’s WA if you would like to get involved in Memory Cafés or volunteer
